UX for Unions

Redesigning union websites at USA Digital Media Agency

Skills Utilized: front-end development, interaction design, content strategy

My role: developed websites using adobe business catalyst, Foundation and Bootstrap frameworks, and Business Catalyst templates. Brought paper form processes and recruiting tools online.

Problem:

Most construction trade associations have websites that are 10+ years old, or they don't have them at all. It was my job to build new websites for our clients, as well as work with them to bring some of their outdated systems online. It was a young agency that was growing quickly, that didn't have any formalize UX procedures or roles beyond a single web developer.

Method:

I strove to build the agencies approach to designing websites as user and client-centered. I sat in on meetings with clients that had previously been only attended by my director, in order to find out what our clients unique needs were. I discussed website pain points and low-tech processes in these conversations with clients. I then incorporated solutions to these issues in the new design, leveraging HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, and JQuery to house application-inquiries, hours-reporting, and other tasks within applications on their new sites.

Results:

Our clients have seen an increase in page visits, recruitment, and workflow efficiency. The agency itself has doubled it's client-base since last year. We now have a database of procedures, resources, and formalized processes for our growing team to leverage.
